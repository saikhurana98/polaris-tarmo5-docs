## What You Need to Learn This Week

You have 4 days. It is ambitious — that is the point.

<div class="skill-header">
  <span class="skill-number">1</span>
  <h3>Soldering Basics</h3>
</div>

You will need to solder motor leads, ESC connections, and battery connectors. Clean joints are non-negotiable — cold solder joints will fail under vibration.

<ul class="resource-list">
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qps9woUGkvI"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> Soldering Basics for Beginners <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vL4DuoKGquk"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> How to Solder Wires Together <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rK38rpUy568&amp;t=167s"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> Soldering Crash Course <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-grid">
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> Tin both surfaces before joining</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> Heat the joint, not the solder</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> A good joint is shiny and concave, not blobby</div>
</div>

<div class="skill-header">
  <span class="skill-number">3</span>
  <h3>Hand Tools &amp; Fastener Basics</h3>
</div>

This sounds simple. It is not. Stripped screws and cross-threaded nylock nuts will cost you hours.

<ul class="resource-list">
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_SBGfMZhRd8"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> How to Use a Screwdriver Properly <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K1_MbZunjOQ"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> Understanding Nylock Nuts <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKsVUTRPM3k"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> Hand Tools Basics for Beginners <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-grid">
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Preventing cross-threading:</strong> Start every bolt by hand (2–3 turns) before using a tool. If it resists, back it out and try again.</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Nylock nuts:</strong> Self-locking by design. The resistance is the nylon insert doing its job. Do not force them with power tools.</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Screwdriver fit:</strong> Use the correct size. A Phillips #2 is not a Phillips #1. Wrong size = stripped head.</div>
</div>

<div class="skill-header">
  <span class="skill-number">4</span>
  <h3>Software Tuning with RadioMaster EdgeTX</h3>
</div>

Your car will not drive straight out of the box. You need to configure your transmitter to set endpoints, trims, and channel mappings.

<ul class="resource-list">
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xvDJDPg2Keg"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> EdgeTX Basics for Beginners <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jNbBdUMN-c"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> RadioMaster Setup Guide <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
  <li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CrXZO9HMGko"><span class="resource-icon">▶</span> EdgeTX Configuration Walkthrough <span class="resource-tag">YouTube</span></a></li>
  <li><a href="https://edgetx.org/documents"><span class="resource-icon">📚</span> EdgeTX Official Documentation <span class="resource-tag">Docs</span></a></li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-grid">
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> Channel mapping (steering, throttle)</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> Endpoint adjustment (limit servo travel to prevent binding)</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> Trim settings (center your steering)</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> Failsafe (car must stop if signal is lost)</div>
</div>

<div class="callout callout-critical">
  <div class="callout-title">⚠ Critical: Controller Profiles &amp; Backup</div>
  <p>The controllers are <strong>shared equipment</strong> across all six teams. Every team is <strong>required</strong> to set up their own profile on each controller they use.</p>
  <p><strong>You must:</strong></p>
  <ul>
    <li>Create and name your team's profile clearly</li>
    <li><strong>Back up your profile</strong> after every tuning session — either from the SD card or via USB cable (look up the exact procedure for your controller model)</li>
    <li><strong>Double-verify all parameters</strong> before every run — check your channel mappings, endpoints, trims, and failsafe settings</li>
  </ul>
  <p><strong>Why this matters:</strong> Because controllers are shared, there is nothing stopping another team from accidentally — or intentionally — modifying your configuration. It is very difficult to lock individual profiles with a key. The organising team <strong>will not be held responsible</strong> if your qualifying run fails because someone changed your config.</p>
  <p>This system relies on <strong>honour among teams. Rivals, not enemies.</strong> Embrace that.</p>
  <p>Treat your EdgeTX configuration as equally important as the physical build of your car. A perfectly built Tarmo5 with bad software config will not complete a single lap.</p>
</div>

<ul class="checklist">
  <li><span class="check-box"></span> All 3D-printed parts inspected and cleaned</li>
