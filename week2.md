## This Week's Challenge

Complete a session of **3 timed laps in succession** during Thursday's official 1-hour session (**6 PM – 7 PM**, 2nd floor track).

You have that one hour to set your laps. There are no make-ups or extensions — if you don't get it done within the session window, it doesn't count. This is no longer just about finishing. Your laps will be **timed**, and consistency matters. The car must complete all 3 laps without stopping, without being touched, and without leaving the track.

### No Immediate Disqualification — But Points Matter

Unlike Week 1's pass/fail format, this week introduces a **points-based rubric**. Teams that fail to complete 3 timed laps in succession will **not** be disqualified outright. However, you **will lose points** on the rubric.

These points accumulate across weeks. Fall too far behind and the math stops working in your favour. The rubric details will be shared separately — pay attention to it.

## What You Should Focus On

If your car DNF'd last week, you already know what needs fixing. Here's what separates a car that finishes from one that doesn't:

<div class="tip-grid">
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Reliability over speed:</strong> A slow car that finishes 3 laps beats a fast car that breaks on lap 2. Prioritise consistency.</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Tighten everything:</strong> Vibration loosens screws. Go through every fastener. Check motor mounts, wheel hubs, and steering linkage.</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Re-check your soldering:</strong> If a joint looked questionable last week, redo it now. Cold joints fail under sustained load.</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>EdgeTX tuning:</strong> Reduce your throttle endpoint if the car is too fast to control. Smooth inputs matter more than raw speed.</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Battery management:</strong> Run your laps on a fully charged battery. Know your run time so the car doesn't die mid-session.</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Practice on the actual track:</strong> The track is open at all times. Run your car there before Thursday — not just on a flat floor. Learn the corners, find the grip limits, and sort out problems in practice, not during your timed session.</div>
  <div class="tip"><span class="tip-icon">✓</span> <strong>Have spare parts ready:</strong> Print duplicates of fragile parts (steering knuckles, wheel hubs, bumper mounts). A 5-minute part swap beats a DNF because you had nothing to replace a broken piece with.</div>
</div>
