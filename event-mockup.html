Flow of the Event

How the Hour Unfolds

Each session runs to a tight rhythm. Six teams, the clock, and a track full of crash potential. Here is what to expect.

01
Doors Open · 5:45 PM
Audience seating along the marked perimeter. Teams roll in their cars, complete their final tech checks, and warm up batteries. The track is open for inspection.
02
Race Briefing · 5:55 PM
Organisers walk through the format, track limits, and call order. Teams confirm their drivers, spotters, and pit crews. The clock is set.
03
Runs Begin · 6:00 PM
Teams take the track in rotation. Each car must complete 5 consecutive timed laps without stopping. Lap times are called live as the car crosses the line.
04

If time permits, teams may run again to improve their best 5-lap session. The fastest complete 5-lap run is the one that scores.
05
Session Ends · 7:00 PM
Sharp. Whatever has been timed by 7:00 PM stands as the result. Standings go up on the board immediately after.
Rules & Regulations

How Scoring Works

Clean, simple, public. Every team races against the same rubric. The track is the truth.

Out Lap + 5 Timed Laps
Each session is one Out Lap followed by 5 Timed Laps, run in unbroken succession. The Out Lap doesn't score — it's there to settle the car. The five that follow are the ones being clocked.
Best Average Lap Time Wins
Each lap is individually timed. The team's score is the average across the five laps. Lowest average wins. Consistency beats raw speed.
Up to 5 Faults Per Session
A fault counts toward your budget across the full session (Out Lap + 5 Timed Laps). Two kinds of fault count:

1. Track Limit Violation — all four tyres leave the track surface.
2. Track Reset — the car becomes undrivable (stuck, flipped, jammed) and needs a hands-on reset to track position. Counts even if the car never left the track.

A 6th fault ends the session.
The Ramp Jump
If the car fails to land within the track after the ramp, you must restart before the ramp and re-attempt the jump. The session timer keeps running. The failed jump itself counts as a fault.
Multiple Attempts Allowed
Within the 1-hour window, teams may run more than once. Only the best complete 5-lap session counts toward standings.
Only the Final Counts
Wednesday's Final is the only scored session. Monday's Semi Finals run the same format under the same rules — same audience, same pressure — but the time set there does not determine the winner. The Final decides it.

Arrive by 5:55 PM. Doors open at 5:45. Runs start sharp at 6:00. Late arrivals get whatever space is left.
Stay behind the cones. The track perimeter is marked. The cars are fast and unpredictable — keep yourself and your bag clear of the racing surface.
